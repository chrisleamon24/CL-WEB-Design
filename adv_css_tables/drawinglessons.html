Learning to Draw

When you think of drawing it can be intimidating. Especially, if your point of view is that only people with a natural talent or artisic skills can draw. Honestly that isn't the case. Anyone can draw, the only question is how much time and effort do you want to apply. All it takes is a vision of what you want to put on the paper and practice the fundamentals until you reach your own expectations. It doesn't matter what others think it's about how you judge your own artwork!

The Seven Principles of Drawing

Here are some of the basic principles of drawing that can really help you on the journey

  • Balance - The distribution of visual weight of objects, colors, texture, and space.
  • Contrast - This helps create movement, tension, and focus on the drawing.
  • Emphasis - This is to attract the viewers attention to the main point or subject of your drawing.
  • Rhythym - The idea here is keep a flow with your drawing, some examples could be the theme is calm and relaxing or alive and energetic.
  • Pattern - This could be repeating shapes or colors, for instance a checkerboard has the same pattern.
  • Unity - Sperate parts of your drawing that is working together like a sense of harmony.
  • Variety - Here you will use different sizes, shapes, and colors in your drawing.
